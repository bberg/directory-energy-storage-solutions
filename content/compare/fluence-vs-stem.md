## Which Should You Choose?

### Choose Fluence if:
- You need hardware and software from a single provider
- Your project is utility-scale or large commercial
- You want a configurable, end-to-end ecosystem

### Choose Stem if:
- You want to optimize storage with software-first AI
- You manage a portfolio of clean energy assets
- Hardware is already in place or sourced separately

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Fluence a competitor to Stem?
They overlap on software but start from different places. Fluence is a full-stack hardware-and-software provider, while Stem is a software-first optimization platform, so they compete most directly on the software and optimization layer.

### Can I use Stem with Fluence hardware?
Stem is a software-first platform focused on monitoring, optimizing, and controlling clean energy portfolios, while Fluence offers its own digital applications with its systems. Compatibility depends on the specific project; confirm directly with each vendor.

### Which is better for a commercial storage project?
If you want a single partner for hardware and software, Fluence's ecosystem covers it. If you need advanced AI optimization over storage you are sourcing or already own, Stem's software-first approach is a strong fit.
