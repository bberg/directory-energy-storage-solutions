## Energy Vault Overview

A major storage company offering battery, gravity, and hydrogen storage options, plus advanced software and operations and consulting services, with a portfolio aimed at flexible, long-duration applications.

**Best For:**
- Projects exploring non-lithium long-duration options
- Utilities wanting multiple storage technologies
- Buyers who value bundled software and operations

**Key Strengths:**
- Diverse technology portfolio (gravity, hydrogen, battery)
- Advanced software and operations services
- Configurable, design-build delivery

## ESS, Inc. Overview

BESS systems for enterprise and utilities built on iron flow battery chemistry, focused on consistent battery output over prolonged durations.

**Best For:**
- Long-duration applications needing repeatable cycling
- Enterprise and utility buyers favoring flow chemistry
- Projects prioritizing abundant, non-lithium materials

**Key Strengths:**
- Iron flow chemistry for sustained output
- Consistent performance over long durations
- Design-build delivery for enterprise and utility scale

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do Energy Vault and ESS, Inc. differ technologically?
Energy Vault offers a portfolio that includes gravity and hydrogen storage as well as batteries, while ESS, Inc. specializes in iron flow batteries designed for consistent output over long durations.

### Which is better for long-duration storage?
Both target long-duration needs. ESS, Inc.'s iron flow chemistry is purpose-built for sustained, repeatable discharge, while Energy Vault provides multiple long-duration technologies to match different project requirements.

### Are these the only long-duration options?
No. Larger lithium-ion systems from providers like Fluence and Wärtsilä can also be configured for longer durations. See our [long-duration storage guide](/best/long-duration-energy-storage) for more.
