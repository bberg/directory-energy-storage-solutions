## Long-Duration Storage Technologies

- **Iron flow batteries** - Use abundant iron-based electrolyte for long, repeatable discharge.
- **Gravity storage** - Raise and lower mass to store and release potential energy.
- **Hydrogen storage** - Convert electricity to hydrogen for long-term energy holding.
- **Scaled lithium-ion** - Larger capacity ratios extend lithium systems toward longer durations.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is long-duration energy storage?
Long-duration energy storage (LDES) generally refers to systems that can discharge for many hours, often 8 hours or more, and in some cases days. It complements shorter-duration lithium-ion by bridging longer gaps in renewable generation.

### Which technologies are used for long-duration storage?
Common approaches include flow batteries (such as ESS, Inc.'s iron flow chemistry), gravity storage, and hydrogen storage, all of which Energy Vault and ESS, Inc. represent in this directory. Lithium-ion can also be configured for longer durations.

### Why does long-duration storage matter?
As grids add more solar and wind, longer-duration storage helps shift surplus daytime energy into evenings and through low-generation periods, improving reliability and reducing curtailment.
